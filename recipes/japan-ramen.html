Ebi Ramen: Japanese classic dish

Ebi ramen is a delicious, quick-to-prepare meal featuring a savory shrimp-infused broth. The key is using a flavorful base and cooking the shrimp just right.

Ingredients

For the Broth base

  • 1 Tbsp sesame oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 2 Tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp chili paste (like Srirarcha or Sambal Oelek, optional, to taste)

For the Ramen

  • 8 oz ramen noodles (discard seasoning packets if using instant)
  • 1/2 lb peeled and deveined raw shrimp
  • 1 cup baby bok choy, leaves halved lengthwise

Suggested topings

  • Soft-boiled eggs, halved
  • Chopped green onions
  • Toasted sesame seeds
  • Nori (seaweed) strips

Instructions

  1. Prepare the broth: In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the sesame oil over medium heat. Add the garlic and ginger, cooking for about a minute until fragrant. Pour in the broth, soy sauce, and chili paste (if using). Bring the mixture to a simmer and let it cook for 10-15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  2. Cook noodles and shrimp: In a separate pot, cook the ramen noodles according to package instructions. In the last 3 minutes of the broth simmering time, add the raw shrimp and bok choy to the broth and cook until the shrimp is pink and opaque and the bok choy is tender.
  3. Assemble and serve: Divide the cooked noodles among serving bowls. Ladle the hot ebi broth, shrimp, and bok choy over the noodles.
  4. Garnish: Top each bowl with the halved soft-boiled eggs, chopped green onions, sesame seeds, and nori strips. Serve immediately and enjoy your homemade ebi ramen.
